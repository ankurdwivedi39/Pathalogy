ESR Test

Measures the **Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)**—the speed at which red blood cells settle in a tube. A non-specific indicator used to detect and monitor **inflammation** or tissue damage in the body.

What It Measures

  • **ESR Value:** Measures how quickly red blood cells clump and fall to the bottom of a test tube, which is accelerated by inflammatory proteins.
  • Used for monitoring inflammatory conditions like Rheumatoid Arthritis, Lupus, and certain chronic infections.
  • It is a non-specific test, meaning it detects inflammation but not the exact source.
