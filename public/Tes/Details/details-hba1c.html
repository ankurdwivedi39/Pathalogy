HbA1c (Glycosylated Hemoglobin)

The gold standard test for assessing long-term blood sugar control. HbA1c provides an average of blood glucose levels over the preceding 2 to 3 months.

What It Measures

  • **Glycosylated Hemoglobin (HbA1c):** Measures the percentage of hemoglobin that has glucose attached to it.
  • Provides a highly reliable average blood sugar result, unaffected by temporary diet changes or stress.
  • Used for diabetes diagnosis, pre-diabetes screening, and monitoring treatment efficacy.

Preparation Guidelines

  • **Fasting:** **NOT required** for this test. You can eat and drink as normal.
  • **Frequency:** Recommended every 3 to 6 months for diabetic patients.
  • **Consultation:** If the result is above 6.5%, consult your doctor for further evaluation.
