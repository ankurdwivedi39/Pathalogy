High-Sensitivity CRP (hs-CRP)

An advanced test that measures low levels of **C-Reactive Protein (CRP)** in the blood. It is used as a predictor for future cardiovascular events (heart attack and stroke) and systemic inflammation.

What It Measures

  • **hs-CRP:** Measures inflammation at very low levels (mg/L).
  • High levels indicate inflammation in the arteries, which is a major risk factor for heart disease, independent of cholesterol.
  • Used by doctors to refine risk assessment, particularly for individuals with metabolic syndrome.

Preparation Guidelines

  • **Fasting:** Fasting is **not required**, but is often recommended if combined with a Lipid Profile.
  • **Activity:** Avoid **vigorous physical activity** for 48 hours prior to the test, as muscle strain can temporarily elevate CRP levels.
  • **Consultation:** Interpretation is based on risk categories (<1.0 mg/L = Low Risk; 1.0–3.0 mg/L = Average Risk; >3.0 mg/L = High Risk).
