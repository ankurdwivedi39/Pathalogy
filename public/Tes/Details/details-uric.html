Uric Acid Test

Measures the level of uric acid in the blood, a breakdown product of purines. High levels are the primary indicator for **Gout** and may suggest kidney stone risk.

What It Measures

  • **Serum Uric Acid:** Measures the concentration of uric acid dissolved in the bloodstream.
  • High levels (hyperuricemia) can lead to crystal formation in joints (gout).
  • Used for diagnosis and monitoring of gout and certain kidney stone formations.

Preparation Guidelines

  • **Fasting:** Fasting is **not generally required**, but is sometimes recommended to avoid transient elevation after a high-purine meal. Check if other tests in your order require fasting.
  • **Avoid:** Avoid alcohol and high-purine foods (red meat, seafood, certain vegetables) for 24 hours before the test, as they can temporarily increase levels.
  • **Medication:** Do not stop taking gout or diuretic medication unless instructed by your doctor.
